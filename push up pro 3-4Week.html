üí° Tip: Rest 60-90 sec between strength sets. Control every rep!
1. Weighted Push-Ups
  • Place a backpack with books (or weight plate) on your upper back.
  • Hands slightly wider than shoulders, body straight.
  • Lower chest to floor, elbows at 45¬∞, push up explosively.
Goal: Build raw pushing strength for advanced variations.
2. Archer Push-Ups
  • Start in wide push-up position (hands 2x shoulder-width).
  • Shift weight to one side, bend that elbow while keeping the other arm straight.
  • Lower until chest is near the bent-arm side‚Äôs hand, then push up.
Key Tip: Keep hips level‚Äîno twisting!
3. Negative Diamond Push-Ups
  • Hands close together under chest (thumbs/index fingers form a diamond).
  • Lower chest to hands VERY slowly (5 seconds), then drop knees to reset.
Goal: Strengthen triceps for one-arm push-up prep.
4. Pike Push-Ups
  • Form an upside-down "V" with hips high, hands and feet on floor.
  • Bend elbows to lower head toward floor, then push back up.
Tip: Works shoulders like a handstand push-up!

1. Spiderman Push-Ups
  • Start in push-up position.
  • As you lower, bring one knee to the same-side elbow.
  • Push up and return leg. Alternate sides.
Tip: Engages obliques + hip flexors.
2. Hanging Leg Raises
  • Hang from a bar (or use parallel bars).
  • Raise legs straight up to 90¬∞, lower slowly.
  • No bar? Do lying leg raises on the floor.
Goal: Strengthen lower abs for push-up stability.
3. Russian Twists
  • Sit on floor, knees bent, lean back 45¬∞.
  • Hold weight (or clasp hands), rotate torso side-to-side.
Tip: Keep feet off floor for extra challenge.

1. Pyramid Push-Ups
  • Do 1 push-up, rest 15 sec; 2 push-ups, rest 15 sec; up to 5, then back down.
  • Example: 1-2-3-4-5-4-3-2-1 = 25 total push-ups.
Goal: Build endurance without sacrificing form.
2. Superman Hold
  • Lie face down, arms extended overhead like Superman flying.
  • Lift arms, chest, and legs off floor, hold for time.
Tip: Strengthens lower back to balance core work.

1. Dragon Flags (Beginner Version)
  • Lie on back, grip a sturdy object behind head (e.g., bed frame).
  • Lift legs and hips toward ceiling, then lower slowly without touching floor.
  • Too hard? Bend knees or lower only halfway.
Goal: Ultimate core control for advanced push-ups.
2. Shoulder Dislocates
  • Hold a band/broomstick wide, arms straight.
  • Slowly raise overhead and behind back, then return.
Tip: Improves shoulder mobility for archer push-ups.
