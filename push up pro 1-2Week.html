üìò Exercise Explanations

1. Wall Push-Ups
  • Stand facing a wall, arms out straight.
  • Place palms flat on the wall at chest level.
  • Bend your elbows and bring your chest toward the wall.
  • Push back.
Goal: Build muscle memory + warm up shoulders.
2. Incline Push-Ups (table/bed)
  • Place hands on a table, bed, or bench.
  • Keep your body straight like a plank.
  • Lower chest slowly, elbows 45¬∞ from your body.
  • Push back up.
Key Tip: Don't let hips sag or butt stick up.
3. Negative Push-Ups
  • Start in full push-up position.
  • Lower your chest very slowly (3‚Äì5 seconds) to the floor.
  • Rest at bottom, then reset.
Goal: Train strength in the hardest part of push-ups.
4. Knee Push-Ups
  • Start in push-up position with knees on ground.
  • Keep body straight from knees to head.
  • Lower chest, elbows at 45¬∞.
  • Push up.
Tip: Tighten your core, no arching or sagging.
5. Wall Hold (Plank Position)
  • Face a wall, place hands in push-up position.
  • Step back slightly and hold like a standing plank.
Goal: Strengthens posture muscles for better push-ups.
6. Plank
  • Forearms on ground, elbows under shoulders.
  • Body in a straight line.
  • Tighten abs and glutes.
Tip: Breathe slowly. Don't hold your breath.
7. Dead Bug
  • Lie on back, arms and legs in the air (knees bent 90¬∞).
  • Lower right arm + left leg together slowly.
  • Return to start. Switch sides.
Goal: Keep your lower back pressed to the floor.
8. Leg Raises (Lying)
  • Lie on your back, hands under your hips.
  • Raise legs up, lower slowly without dropping.
  • Stop just before heels touch floor.
Tip: Engage abs, don't arch your back.
9. Side Plank
  • Lie on your side, elbow under shoulder.
  • Lift hips so body is in a straight line.
  • Hold. Switch sides.
10. Mountain Climbers
  • Start in push-up position.
  • Bring one knee toward your chest.
  • Quickly switch legs like running in place.
Tip: Keep body straight and core tight. Move fast.
11. Hold Top Push-Up Position
  • Get in full push-up position and hold without moving.
  • Squeeze glutes and abs.
Goal: Build stability and control.
12. Plank to Push-Up (aka Up-Downs)
  • Start in forearm plank.
  • Push up one arm at a time to push-up position.
  • Lower back to forearms, alternate leading arm.
Tip: Keep hips steady. Don't rock too much.
